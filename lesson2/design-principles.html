Fitts’ Law

Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints

www.churchofjesuschrist.org

Fitts' law states that the amount of time required to move the mouse cursor to a target area is proportional to the distance to the target divided by the size of the target. This means that the longer the distance and the smaller the target's size, the longer it takes. The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints' website is a good example of this. The website uses very large buttons for featured articles and the distance between them is very short. Making it easier and faster to browse through the website.

Hick’s Law

IBM

www.ibm.com/

Hick's Law examines the relationship between the number of stimuli present and the individual's reaction time to any given stimulus. This means that if you reduce the number of stimuli you'll get a faster decision-making process. The IBM website is a good example of this. While IBM offers a lot of option, they applied the "K.I.S.S" principle on their menu in order to keep things short and simple. When entering into each sub-menu, many more options are presented. Sometimes, there is no avoiding complexity, and the IBM website shows that while still combining it with Hick's Law.

Visual Hierarchy

Squarespace

www.squarespace.com

Visual Hierarchy refers to the arrangament and presentation of elements in a way that denotes importance while influencing the order in which it is perceived by end user. In web design, the F-Pattern explains the way our eyes scan a website. Studies have shown that we read in an F shaped pattern while browsing the internet. With a well-executed design, the most important information is placed within an F pattern allowing the user to quickly find the information they need. The Squarespace website is a good example of this. You can easily scan the images and catch keywords with ease.
